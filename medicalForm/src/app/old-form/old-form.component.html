INSTRUCTIONS

Please read carefully - Thank you

You or your company have requested a Personal Track Safety Medical, your examination will ensure that you meet the requirements to work in the rail industry in line with network rail standard NR/L2/OHS/00124

The medical will consist of several observations and tests in which you should disclose any pre-existing conditions to our clinical team prior to the examination. Conditions such as epilepsy, diabetes, or any form of disorder to your balance, co-ordination, blood pressure, heart disease, obesity, sleep disorder, mental health or depression will need to be disclosed.

You will also be asked to prove your identity with a photographic ID. Please show one of the following to the medical practitioner.

  • 1. Valid Sentinel Card
  • 2. Photographic driving licence
  • 3. Passport
  • 4. Employer ID card, Student card, HMP ID card

N.B You cannot undertake any medical assessment unless you produce the correct form of ID listed above.

The medical examination will consist of an examination of your eyesight (distance) therefore, if needed, please bring spectacles with you. You will be tested for colour vision deficiency and your blood pressure will be measured. A hearing test will also be carried out using an audiometer, in order to pass a PTS medical, you will need to pass this part of the examination.

If you are not having a drug screen test, you will still be asked to provide a sample of urine in order to ensure that it does not contain glucose (sugar) which would indicate that diabetes is developing. Please note that if on the day the testing equipment finds that you have high blood pressure or you have glucose in your urine you will be issued with a letter for you to take to your GP for further investigation of the results. A medical certificate for a reduced level of working or prohibiting trackside working might be issued whilst these investigations take place. This will not stop you from working but it is important you action any advice given.

Following the examination, the certificate will be uploaded onto the sentinel system by Platinum Rail Services which creates an online certificate once you have a sentinel profile. Occasionally it may be necessary for your sentinel card to display a red triangle which indicates you should not work alone and will need to be accompanied at all times due to medical restrictions or a blue circle which indicates colour vision deficiency and in turn will prevent you from carrying out certain roles on the railway. Your primary sponsor will have access to your medical certificate via sentinel and no paper copy will be given.
